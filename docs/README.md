## Project Management Processes at OctoAcme

OctoAcme follows a structured, five-phase lifecycle approach to project management that emphasizes customer value, iterative delivery, and clear ownership. The methodology spans **project initiation** (validating ideas through a lightweight one-pager), **planning** (breaking work into shippable increments with clear acceptance criteria), **execution** (delivering through daily standups and small pull requests with automated testing), **release and deployment** (standardizing how features reach production), and **retrospectives** (capturing learnings to drive continuous improvement).

The organization defines clear roles with distinct responsibilities: **Product Managers** own the vision and prioritize the backlog; **Project Managers** coordinate delivery and manage risks; **Developers** implement features and collaborate on design; and **QA/Testing** validates quality. This distributed ownership is reinforced through a consistent communication cadence—weekly syncs between PM and Product Lead, twice-weekly standups, and monthly stakeholder updates—ensuring transparency and reducing single-person dependency risk.

Quality and testing are embedded throughout delivery, not treated as an afterthought. OctoAcme requires unit tests for new logic, integration tests where applicable, end-to-end smoke tests before release, and security scanning in CI pipelines. Risk management is equally proactive: risks are captured in a formal Risk Register and escalated through a three-level path (team → PM → Product Lead → Sponsor) to ensure blockers are resolved efficiently.

Finally, OctoAcme emphasizes learning through structured retrospectives held after each sprint or milestone. By grounding all projects in documented processes, maintaining a single source of truth for status and decisions, and fostering psychological safety, OctoAcme enables consistent, repeatable execution while reducing friction for new team members.

1. **Start here:** Read the [Project Management Overview](octoacme-project-management-overview.md) to understand the principles and roles.

5. **Learning and improving:** Participate in retrospectives using guidance in the [Retrospective & Continuous Improvement](octoacme-retrospective-and-continuous-improvement.md) guide.

## Key Principles

- **Customer-first:** Prioritize customer value and usability in all decisions.
- **Iterative delivery:** Deliver small, testable increments rather than big-bang releases.
- **Clear ownership:** Each project has named Project Manager and Product Lead roles.
- **Data-informed:** Measure impact and iterate based on evidence.
- **Psychological safety:** Encourage feedback, learning, and blameless retrospectives.
